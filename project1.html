Acima Mobile App

Turning one-time customers into loyal, repeat buyers — and driving 100%+ year-over-year growth for three consecutive years.

Company Acima
Role Product Manager → Group PM
Focus Mobile, Retention, Growth
Timeline 2023 – 2025

3M+ Total App Downloads

100%+ YoY Growth (3 years running)

The Problem

Acima had customers — but not repeat customers. After their first lease, most users had little reason to come back. The mobile app was an underutilized channel with huge potential to drive re-engagement, build brand loyalty, and convert one-time shoppers into long-term customers.

The challenge: how do you make a lease-to-own app something people actually want to open again?

My Role

I owned this product end-to-end as the sole Product Manager on the mobile app. I was responsible for strategy, roadmap, working with engineering and design, and aligning stakeholders on priorities. Over time, as the app grew in scale and complexity, I transitioned into a Group PM role — hiring, onboarding, and managing the PM who took over day-to-day ownership of the app.

"I went from building the product to building the team that builds the product — and making sure the vision stayed intact through that transition."

The Approach

  • Customer-first research: Dug into why customers weren't coming back. Mapped the post-lease customer journey to find drop-off points and unmet needs.
  • Re-engagement features: Prioritized features that gave returning customers a reason to open the app — lease management tools, payment visibility, and personalized offers.
  • Iterative shipping: Ran fast, focused release cycles. Measured each release against retention and re-engagement metrics, not just download counts.
  • Cross-functional alignment: Worked closely with marketing, engineering, and customer success to ensure the in-app experience matched the broader customer lifecycle.
  • Scaled through leadership: As the app matured, I shifted focus to mentoring a PM to own execution — freeing me to focus on strategy and organizational alignment.

The Results

The strategy worked. By focusing on repeat customer engagement rather than pure acquisition, the app became a retention engine:

  • Grew to 3 million+ downloads and 500,000 monthly active users
  • Achieved 100%+ year-over-year growth in 2023, 2024, and 2025 — three consecutive years of doubling
  • Transitioned from a utility app to a genuine customer engagement platform
  • Successfully scaled the team, promoting PM ownership while maintaining product direction

What I Learned

The biggest lesson: growth metrics follow engagement metrics. When we stopped optimizing for downloads and started optimizing for why a customer would come back, the downloads followed. The app only scaled because the core experience earned it.

Leading this product through the transition from IC to manager also taught me how to hold strategy while empowering others to own execution — which is exactly what Director-level leadership requires.
