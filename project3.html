The Problem

We had customers ‚Äî but we weren't listening to them in any structured way. There was no centralized feedback system, no NPS tracking, and no consistent process for turning what customers told us into product decisions. Different teams had fragments of information, but there was no unified view of customer sentiment and no way to act on it systematically.

The result was a product built largely on assumptions. We needed to change that.

"You can't build a product people love if you're not actually listening to them. We didn't just need a survey ‚Äî we needed a culture of feedback."

My Role

I led the design and implementation of a comprehensive customer feedback strategy ‚Äî from selecting the right tools and designing the feedback touchpoints, to building the cross-functional processes that ensured insights were actually used. This touched Customer Success, Engineering, Design, Marketing, and Executive Leadership.

The Challenges

  • Unifying fragmented sources: Feedback lived in support tickets, CS notes, ad-hoc surveys, and individual team channels. Pulling this together into a coherent picture required both tooling and process changes.
  • Making data actionable: Raw feedback is noise without a framework to interpret it. We built categorization and prioritization processes so PMs could act on insights rather than drown in them.
  • Getting teams to act on the data: Collecting feedback is easy. Getting engineering, design, and product teams to consistently factor it into roadmap decisions required sustained effort ‚Äî regular syncs, exec-level reporting, and embedding feedback reviews into sprint planning.

The Outcome

The strategy transformed how the organization related to its customers:

  • NPS scores improved as the product began reflecting what customers actually needed rather than internal assumptions
  • Feedback directly influenced the roadmap ‚Äî customer insights became a regular input into prioritization decisions across teams
  • Executive leadership gained reliable visibility into customer sentiment through regular, structured reporting
  • We closed the feedback loop with customers, communicating how their input shaped product changes ‚Äî building trust and improving response rates over time

What I Learned

Feedback systems fail when they stop at collection. The hard work isn't getting customers to fill out a survey ‚Äî it's building the internal processes that ensure someone actually does something with the data. The most valuable thing we built wasn't the tooling. It was the habit of listening.
