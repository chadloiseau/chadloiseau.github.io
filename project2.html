Roadmap Tool Integration

Building a roadmapping practice from zero — and getting an entire organization aligned around a single source of truth.

Tools Airfocus & Roadmunk
Role Evaluation & Selection Lead
Scale Org-Wide (6+ Teams)
Focus Strategy & Tooling

6+ Teams Aligned on One Tool
2 Tools Selected & Implemented

The Problem

There was no formal roadmapping process. Teams were working from scattered spreadsheets, slide decks, and ad-hoc documents — each with their own format, their own prioritization logic, and no easy way to align with stakeholders or leadership on what was being built and why.

The result: misalignment, duplicated effort, and roadmap reviews that were more about reconciling documents than making decisions. We needed a single source of truth, built on a process people would actually use.

"We weren't just buying a tool. We were building a muscle the organization didn't have yet — and the tool needed to support that, not complicate it."

My Role

I led the full evaluation and selection process — from defining requirements, scanning the market, running vendor trials, and building a scoring framework, all the way through to the final recommendation. I worked cross-functionally to gather input from PMs, engineering leads, and executive stakeholders to make sure whatever we chose would actually stick.

The Process

1

Market Scan & RFP

Reviewed the roadmapping tool landscape and issued formal requirements to shortlisted vendors.

2

Stakeholder Input

Collected requirements from PMs, engineering, design, and leadership to ensure broad fit.

3

Trials & Pilots

Ran hands-on pilots with shortlisted tools across real teams with real roadmaps.

4

Scoring Matrix

Built a criteria-based scoring model to evaluate tools objectively across key dimensions.

The Challenges

This wasn't just a procurement exercise — it was a change management project. The real obstacles were human:

  • Resistance to change: Teams had their own ways of working and weren't eager to adopt something new. We addressed this by involving them early in the evaluation rather than handing down a decision.
  • Leadership buy-in: Making the case to invest in dedicated tooling when the status quo "worked" required a clear articulation of cost — the hidden cost of misalignment, duplicated work, and slow reviews.
  • Training and adoption: Selecting a tool is the easy part. Getting 6+ teams to use it consistently required deliberate onboarding, templates, and ongoing support.
  • Budget and procurement: Navigating vendor negotiations and internal budget approval added complexity to the timeline.

The Outcome

We selected and implemented two complementary tools — Airfocus for prioritization and strategic roadmapping, and Roadmunk for stakeholder-facing timeline views. The combination gave us the right tool for the right audience.

  • Replaced a patchwork of spreadsheets and slide decks with a single source of truth used across 6+ teams
  • Stakeholder roadmap reviews became faster and more decision-focused
  • Executive leadership gained clear, consistent visibility into product direction
  • PMs spent less time maintaining roadmaps and more time on strategy

What I Learned

Tool selection is 20% picking the right software and 80% change management. The teams that adopted it fastest were the ones we involved earliest. The lesson I carry forward: when you're building a new practice, inclusion in the process is the adoption strategy.
